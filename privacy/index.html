Chinese Dictionary

Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 26, 2026 · Applies to the Chinese Dictionary Chrome extension and chinesehanzi.app

Chinese Dictionary runs entirely on your device. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. The dictionary and everything you save stay on your computer. We have no servers that receive your data, and we use no third-party analytics or trackers.

What the extension stores (on your device only)

To work as a dictionary and study tool, the extension saves the following in your browser's local storage, on your own computer. None of it is ever sent to us or to anyone else:

  • Saved words and study progress — the flashcard deck you build and its spaced-repetition schedule.
  • Lookup history — words you've looked up, so you can find them again.
  • Settings — your display and behavior preferences (theme, popup options, per-site on/off).

This data lives in your browser's local storage and a local database. It stays on your device and is removed if you clear the extension's data or uninstall the extension.

What the extension does NOT do

  • It does not send your browsing activity, the pages you visit, or the text on those pages to us or any third party.
  • It does not contain analytics, tracking pixels, advertising, or fingerprinting.
  • It makes no network requests to external servers — the entire dictionary (definitions, example sentences, stroke data, character etymology, HSK word lists) is bundled inside the extension and loaded locally.
  • It does not collect names, emails, accounts, location, or payment information.

How lookups work

When you hover over or select Chinese text, the extension reads only that short piece of text and looks it up in the offline dictionary bundled inside the extension. The lookup happens locally in the extension's own background script. The text under your cursor is not stored and not transmitted anywhere.

Permissions

The extension requests the minimum permissions it needs, all used locally:

  • activeTab — to read the current tab's address only when you click the extension icon (for "turn off on this site" and to start the dictionary on that page).
  • storage — to save your deck, history, and settings on your device.
  • scripting — to turn the dictionary on/off on the current page when you press the shortcut or the "Turn on" button.
  • contextMenus — to add the right-click "Look up in Chinese Dictionary" item for selected text.

The extension declares no host permissions and accesses no external websites.

Third parties

None. We do not use third-party services, analytics providers, or advertising networks. No data is shared with anyone because no data leaves your device.

The bundled dictionary content is built from open data sources (such as CC-CEDICT, Tatoeba example sentences, and open stroke/HSK data), credited in the extension's "About" section. These are static data files included in the package — they do not receive any information about you.

Children

This extension is a general-audience language tool. It does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Data retention and deletion

Because all data is stored locally, you are in full control. You can delete saved words and history from within the extension, clear the extension's storage in your browser, or uninstall the extension to remove everything.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the extension handles data — for example, if we ever add optional, privacy-respecting usage analytics in a future version — we will update this policy, update the extension's Chrome Web Store data disclosures, and where required ask for your consent before any data is collected. The "Effective date" above will reflect the latest version.
